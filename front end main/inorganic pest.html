Inorganic Pesticides

1. DDT (Dichloro Diphenyl Trichloroethane)

Source: Synthetic chemical compound (chlorinated hydrocarbon)

Used For: Mosquitoes, flies, crop-eating insects

How It Works: Attacks insect nervous system, causes paralysis and death

2. Malathion

Source: Organophosphate compound

Used For: Aphids, leafhoppers, fruit flies, and mites

How It Works: Stops nerve transmission in insects, causing quick death

3. Carbaryl (Sevin)

Source: Synthetic carbamate chemical

Used For: Beetles, caterpillars, and worms

How It Works: Affects insect's central nervous system

4. Endosulfan

Source: Organochlorine compound (synthetic)

Used For: Aphids, whiteflies, and borers

How It Works: Damages insect nerve cells

5. Copper Sulfate

Source: Inorganic mineral salt (CuSO₄)

Used For: Fungal diseases like blight and mildew

How It Works: Kills fungal spores and prevents spread

6. Sulfur Dust

Source: Elemental sulfur (natural mineral, chemically refined)

Used For: Powdery mildew, rust, and mites

How It Works: Produces sulfur vapors toxic to fungi and pests

7. Zinc Phosphide

Source: Inorganic compound (Zn₃P₂)

Used For: Rodent control (rats, mice)

How It Works: Produces poisonous phosphine gas in stomach of rodents

8. Bordeaux Mixture

Source: Mix of Copper Sulfate + Lime + Water

Used For: Fungal infections in grapes, potatoes, and tomatoes

How It Works: Coats leaves and prevents fungal spore germination

9. Paris Green

Source: Copper acetoarsenite compound

Used For: Leaf-eating caterpillars and beetles

How It Works: Arsenic component acts as stomach poison for pests

10. Lime Sulfur

Source: Calcium polysulfide (from lime + sulfur mix)

Used For: Fungi, mites, and insects

How It Works: Releases sulfur dioxide gas that kills pests and spores
