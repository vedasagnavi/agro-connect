Fertilizers

Organic

1. Compost

Source: Decomposed kitchen waste, leaves, and plant material

Used For: Improving overall soil health and fertility

How It Works: Adds organic matter, increases soil microbes, and improves water retention

2. Vermicompost

Source: Decomposed organic waste processed by earthworms

Used For: All crops for better growth and yield

How It Works: Enriches soil with nutrients, enzymes, and beneficial bacteria

3. Cow Dung Manure

Source: Decomposed cow dung and straw

Used For: General crop nourishment and soil improvement

How It Works: Provides nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium slowly to plants

Source: Crushed animal bones

Used For: Root crops and flowering plants

5. Fish Emulsion

Source: Liquid made from fish waste and oil

Used For: Leafy vegetables and fast-growing plants

How It Works: Provides nitrogen for rapid green growth

6. Seaweed Extract

Source: Marine seaweeds (kelp)

Used For: All crops, especially fruiting plants

How It Works: Boosts growth hormones, improves resistance to stress

7. Green Manure

Source: Plants like clover or sun hemp grown and plowed into soil

Used For: Enriching soil before planting crops

How It Works: Adds nitrogen and organic matter to soil naturally

8. Wood Ash

Source: Burnt plant or wood residues

Used For: Potassium-loving plants like tomatoes and roses

How It Works: Raises soil pH and adds potassium and calcium

9. Banana Peel Fertilizer

Source: Dried or soaked banana peels

Used For: Flowering and fruiting plants

How It Works: High in potassium and phosphorus for better blooms

10. Panchagavya

Source: Mix of cow dung, urine, milk, curd, and ghee

Used For: All types of crops

How It Works: Multi-nutrient organic tonic that boosts plant immunity and growth
